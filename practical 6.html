Tribute to Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose

INTRODUCTION

Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose was one of India's greatest freedom fighters. He played a crucial role in the struggle for independence and is best known for forming the Indian National Army (INA) to fight against British rule.

His famous slogan "Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom!" inspired countless individuals to join the movement for India's liberation. His contributions remain a source of motivation and patriotism.

QUALIFICATION

Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose's qualifications included a first-class degree in philosophy from the University of Calcutta, a successful performance in the Indian Civil Service (ICS) examination (placing fourth), and a strong commitment to India's independence movement.

Born: January 23, 1897
Died: August 18, 1945
